Acetaminophen Level

The Acetaminophen Level test measures the amount of acetaminophen (Tylenol) in the blood. Acetaminophen is a common pain reliever and fever reducer.

Why the test is done: It is ordered to determine if a person has taken too much acetaminophen, which can lead to liver damage.

How the test is performed: A blood sample is taken from a vein in the arm.

Blood Glucose Test

The Blood Glucose Test measures the amount of glucose (sugar) in the blood. It is commonly used to diagnose and monitor diabetes.

Why the test is done: It helps in managing diabetes and assessing blood sugar levels.

How the test is performed: A small amount of blood is drawn, usually from the fingertip, and analyzed with a glucose meter or sent to a laboratory.

Cholesterol Levels

Cholesterol Levels test measures the levels of different types of cholesterol in the blood, including LDL (low-density lipoprotein) and HDL (high-density lipoprotein).

Why the test is done: It assesses the risk of heart disease and helps in managing cholesterol levels through lifestyle changes or medication.

How the test is performed: A blood sample is taken, usually after fasting overnight, to get accurate cholesterol measurements.
